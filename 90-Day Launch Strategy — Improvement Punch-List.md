# 90-Day Rebrand Launch — Improvement Punch-List

Review of the 90-Day Rebrand Launch Strategy. The architecture is sound — phased intent, 9-tile discipline, and the "beautiful, useful, or honest" filter are strong. Below is only what to change, ordered by priority. Items are checkboxes so this doubles as a pre-launch checklist.

---

## 🔴 Must-do before May 11

### 1. Reconcile product names to the real catalog
The deck features products that don't match the live store, so spec/price/bio links won't exist.

- [ ] Replace invented names with real handles everywhere they appear.
  - **In deck:** Lace Fusion VF, Mono Pro, Skin Frontal, Lace Edge 360, HS1V, Mono Edge LF, Lace Edge 180, Skin Frontal XL, Lace Edge 360+
  - **Real catalog (confirm against Shopify):** Lace Pro (French lace, $279), Lace Elite (Swiss lace, $329), Thin Skin Pro ($279), Micro Skin MD ($269), + the rest of the live ranges
- [ ] Affected product spotlights to fix: **Days 5, 10, 20, 24, 30, 31, 34, 45, 52, 62, 66, 76**
- [ ] Day 5 spec accuracy: it says "Swiss lace base" → that's **Lace Elite**, not a "Fusion" product. Match the spec to the correct product.

### 2. Resolve the trust vs. clean-slate tension in the opening
The emails lean on *earned trust* (8 years, thousands of men). The IG opening is engineered as a brand they've never seen. A cold viewer gets vibe-with-no-proof; a returning OneHead follower gets no signal they're in the right place.

- [ ] Keep the Day 1 tile pure wordmark, but add the lineage to the **caption's opening line**:
  > *Eight years ago, this was OneHead Hair. Today it becomes Hair Solutions Co.*
- [ ] This converts the clean slate from "who are these people?" into "they earned this" — same angle as the launch emails.

## 🟡 Smaller catches (polish pass)

- [ ] **Post-mix math doesn't reconcile.** Hero 13 + product 17 + blog 13 + quote 11 + carousels 16 + promo 3 = 73, then "+23–36 reels" → ~96–109, not 90. You're mixing *format* (reel/carousel/static) with *archetype* in one total, so reels double-count. Pick one axis per table.
- [ ] **9-tile rule contradicts the locked opening week.** "One product + one human + one paper hero per row of three" forces each row's exact makeup — but Days 1–3 are brand/brand/founder with no product. Relax to "balanced across any recent 9," or explicitly exempt the opening week.
- [ ] **Father's Day timing (US = Jun 21, 2026).** The gift-guide carousel lands *on* Day 42 (Jun 21) — too late to drive a purchase. Move gift content to **~Jun 14–17** so it converts before the day.
- [ ] **Hashtag typos:** `#hairfornature` (Bundle B) looks like a slip; `#riodelifestyle` isn't a real tag — likely want `#riodejaneiro` + `#lifestyle`.

## Verdict
Nothing here touches the core structure — it holds up. Treat **#1 (product names)** and **#2 (Day 1 lineage line)** as launch-blockers; everything else is polish that can happen in the first week.

*The single most important sentence in the strategy still stands: if a post can't earn its place by being beautiful, useful, or honest — don't post it.*
